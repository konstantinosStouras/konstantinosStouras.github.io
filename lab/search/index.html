Consent to Participate in Research

Title: Searching the Unknown  ·  Principal Investigator: Ilya Morozov  ·  Co-Investigator: Suraj Malladi  ·  Supported by the Kellogg School of Management, Northwestern University.

Key information about this research study

  • The purpose of this study is to investigate how people make choices in complex environments.
  • If you choose to be in the study, you will complete a survey. We expect it to take about 15 minutes.
  • We expect final payments to vary from $3 to $5. You must pass the comprehension test at the beginning in order to receive compensation. Once you pass it, your payment can never fall below $3.
  • You will perform a series of search tasks. In each task you reveal hidden monetary prizes at positions you choose. Your goal is to find the position with the largest prize, but you pay a fixed cost for revealing each prize — so find the best prize you can without revealing too many.
  • The survey is anonymous. Being in this study is voluntary; you may stop at any time. This is a minimal-risk study.

What should I know about participating?

  • Whether or not you take part is up to you, and you can change your mind later.
  • You are not required to answer any question you do not want to answer.

Who can I talk to?

Questions may be directed to Prof. Ilya Morozov (ilya.morozov@kellogg.northwestern.edu) and Prof. Suraj Malladi (suraj.malladi@kellogg.northwestern.edu). This research has been reviewed by an IRB, reachable at (312) 503-9338 or irb@northwestern.edu.
